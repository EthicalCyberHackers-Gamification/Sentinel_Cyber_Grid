Design Recommendation: Add an Analyst Learning Layer

Goal:
Close the learning gap for beginners without disrupting the current SOC simulation flow.

The current mission design is strong:

* terminal-driven investigation
* evidence pinning
* campaign/recon maps
* containment logic
* SOC-style escalation

Do not redesign these systems.

The missing feature is an Analyst Learning Layer that explains key concepts at the moment the player encounters them.

Problem:
Players can follow hints and complete missions without fully understanding:

* why evidence matters
* what acronyms mean
* what commands revealed
* how analysts interpret patterns
* why containment actions are logical

This creates a risk of procedural completion without real learning.

Add:

1. Contextual Analyst Notes
2. Acronym Explainers
3. Optional “explain” command
4. Lightweight SOC verification checkpoints
5. End-of-mission SOC debrief

---

## WHAT STAYS

Keep:

* current terminal-first gameplay
* current mission maps
* current evidence panel
* current pinning system
* current containment logic
* current mission progression
* current CyberCorp operational tone

Do not:

* add long tutorials
* add classroom-style quizzes
* add glossary walls
* interrupt the mission constantly
* redesign the interface
* remove realistic terminology

The goal is supported realism, not simplification.

---

## FEATURE 1 — CONTEXTUAL ANALYST NOTES

When the player discovers major evidence, show a short analyst note explaining why it matters.

Example:
Player discovers SPF FAIL.

Show:

ANALYST NOTE
SPF checks whether a mail server is authorized to send email for a domain. A failure suggests the sender may be spoofed or using unauthorized infrastructure.

Rules:

* Only show notes for important concepts
* Keep notes short
* Show once per concept per mission
* Use operational language
* Do not block gameplay unless needed

---

## FEATURE 2 — ACRONYM EXPLAINERS

For terms like:
SPF, DKIM, IOC, C2, EDR, SIEM, DNS, RDP, SMB, MFA, TTP

Add optional explainability.

Methods:

* hover/click term
* small info icon
* terminal command: explain spf
* terminal command: define c2

Example:

$ explain spf

SPF — Sender Policy Framework
A DNS-based email security check that tells receiving mail systems which servers are allowed to send mail for a domain. SPF failure can indicate spoofing.

Do not replace acronyms with simplified terms. Keep the real acronym, but support it.

---

## FEATURE 3 — INTERPRETATION CHECKPOINTS

Add 1–3 lightweight SOC verification prompts per mission.

These should feel like analyst validation, not school quizzes.

Example:

ANALYST VERIFICATION
Why is this email suspicious?

A. The sender display name says IT Helpdesk
B. SPF failed and the return-path does not match CyberCorp
C. The email arrived in the morning
D. The subject line is short

After answer:
Correct. SPF failure and return-path mismatch suggest the message was sent from infrastructure not authorized by CyberCorp.

Rules:

* Use only at major concept moments
* Keep the tone operational
* Avoid quiz styling
* Explain after answer
* Do not overuse

---

## FEATURE 4 — EVIDENCE INTERPRETATION

Pinned evidence should include:

* what was found
* why it matters
* what conclusion it supports

Example:

Evidence:
Systematic probing of multiple services

Why it matters:
Legitimate applications usually communicate with expected services. A source probing SSH, HTTP, HTTPS, and database ports quickly suggests reconnaissance.

Supports conclusion:
External reconnaissance activity

This helps players learn evidence reasoning, not just evidence collection.

---

## FEATURE 5 — SOC DEBRIEF

At mission completion, add a short debrief:

Sections:

* What happened
* How the attack worked
* Key evidence
* Why the evidence mattered
* What containment did
* Concepts learned
* Real-world analyst takeaway

Example:

SOC DEBRIEF
This was not a normal connection. The external source probed multiple services the host does not normally expose. Combined with unknown WHOIS data and baseline mismatch, the activity matched reconnaissance behavior.

Concepts learned:

* Port scanning
* Baseline comparison
* Threat intelligence lookup
* Evidence correlation
* Reconnaissance detection

---

## DESIGN PRINCIPLE

The learning should follow this loop:

Command
→ Evidence appears
→ Analyst note explains meaning
→ Player validates understanding
→ Evidence is pinned
→ Map reacts
→ Player acts

The game should teach analyst thinking inside the mission flow.

---

## SUCCESS CRITERIA

This feature succeeds if:

* players understand acronyms when they appear
* players know why evidence matters
* players do not blindly follow hints
* mission immersion remains intact
* explanations feel operational, not academic
* end-of-mission debrief reinforces learning
* the simulator becomes practical for beginners without losing realism
