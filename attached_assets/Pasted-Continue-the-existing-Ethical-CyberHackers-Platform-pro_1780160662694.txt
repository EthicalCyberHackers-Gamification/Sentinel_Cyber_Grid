Continue the existing Ethical CyberHackers Platform project.

Current status:

* Mission 1 and Mission 2 work.
* Guided investigative pacing exists.
* Files are revealed one at a time.
* Evidence classification exists.
* Analyst Confidence exists.
* Mission Map exists.
* Threat level, trust score, XP, manager reactions, and investigation board work.
* Everything is frontend-only.

Now implement:

Milestone 28A — Emotional Gameplay Decision Layer

Purpose:
Transform Mission 1 from a simple evidence-classification activity into an emotionally engaging cybersecurity response scenario where student decisions influence how the incident evolves.

Important:
Do NOT redesign the full app.
Do NOT add backend.
Do NOT add AI.
Do NOT add multiplayer.
Do NOT add complex networking.
Do NOT overwhelm students with too many choices.

Core Design Goal:
Students should feel:
“My decisions changed the investigation.”

NOT:
“I clicked through a lesson.”

---

1. ADD BLUE TEAM DECISION MOMENTS

---

At important investigation moments, pause the normal flow briefly and require a Blue Team operational decision.

Only ONE decision panel should appear at a time.

The decision panel should temporarily become the main focus of the screen.

Background UI should dim slightly.

---

2. FIRST DECISION MOMENT

---

Trigger:
After student correctly identifies suspicious_file.txt as Critical Threat Evidence.

Show panel:

BLUE TEAM RESPONSE REQUIRED

Text:
“A workstation appears to be involved in a credential harvesting attempt. What should Blue Team do next?”

Options:
A. Escalate immediately to lead analyst
B. Continue gathering evidence silently
C. Isolate the workstation
D. Ignore for now

---

3. DECISION CONSEQUENCES

---

Each decision should create believable operational consequences.

A. Escalate immediately
Result:

* Manager Trust increases
* Threat Level stabilizes
* Blue Team response logged

Manager:
“Good judgment. Early escalation helps contain credential attacks.”

B. Continue gathering evidence silently
Result:

* Analyst Confidence increases slightly
* Small risk escalation occurs later

Later event:
[RED TEAM ACTIVITY]
Additional suspicious outbound activity detected.

Manager:
“More evidence helps, but delayed containment can increase operational risk.”

C. Isolate workstation
Result:

* Threat Level decreases
* Containment Progress increases
* Some evidence collection opportunities reduce slightly

Manager:
“Workstation isolated. Threat spread reduced.”

D. Ignore for now
Result:

* Threat Level increases
* Incident Pressure rises
* Additional adversary event triggered

Manager:
“We cannot ignore possible credential theft activity.”

Do NOT harshly punish the player.
This is about tension and learning.

---

4. ADD CONTAINMENT PROGRESS

---

Add compact meter:

Containment Progress

Range:
0–100%

Correct operational decisions increase progress.

Threat escalation events may slow or reduce progress slightly.

Mission success should feel like:
“Threat contained.”

---

5. ADD DECISION DELAY TENSION

---

After a student selects an option:

Show:
“Submitting Blue Team recommendation...”

Wait:
700–1400 ms

Then:

* manager response appears
* threat/containment updates occur
* optional adversary activity appears

Purpose:
Create anticipation and emotional tension.

---

6. ADD OPERATIONAL CONSEQUENCE EVENTS

---

Examples:

Correct containment:
[BLUE TEAM ACTION]
Workstation isolation successful.

Delayed response:
[RED TEAM ACTIVITY]
Additional phishing attempt detected.

Strong escalation:
[INCIDENT UPDATE]
Lead analyst reviewing containment request.

These should use the existing toast/event system.

---

7. ADD MINI INCIDENT TIMELINE

---

Add a compact Investigation Timeline section.

Example:
09:12 Incident detected
09:14 Analyst assigned
09:16 Suspicious file identified
09:18 Containment action initiated

Keep compact.
Only latest major events visible.

---

8. ADD MANAGER TRUST FEEDBACK

---

Do not only update numbers silently.

Add emotional framing.

Examples:
“Manager confidence increasing.”
“Lead analyst requesting stronger evidence.”
“Blue Team coordination improving.”

This should appear as:

* toast
* manager message
* subtle status update

---

9. KEEP UI SIMPLE

---

Do NOT:

* show multiple decisions simultaneously
* add giant panels
* add timers everywhere
* add large dashboards
* create complicated strategy systems

Only ONE important decision should demand attention at a time.

---

10. MISSION OUTCOME VARIATIONS

---

Mission 1 should now end with slightly different summaries depending on operational decisions.

Possible outcomes:

Excellent Containment
“Threat contained before additional spread.”

Delayed Containment
“Threat contained after additional suspicious activity was detected.”

Weak Investigation
“Incident escalated with incomplete evidence.”

Do NOT fail the mission completely.

---

11. PRESERVE EXISTING SYSTEMS

---

Do not break:

* terminal commands
* guided file reveal
* reasoning prompts
* investigation board
* evidence classification
* hints
* threat level
* trust score
* XP
* mission completion
* scorecards
* mission map
* local progress save
* Mission 2

---

## GOAL

Mission 1 should now feel like:

investigate
→ interpret
→ decide
→ consequence
→ containment
→ mission outcome

instead of:

open file
→ classify
→ continue

The experience should become emotionally engaging without becoming visually cluttered or mechanically overwhelming.
