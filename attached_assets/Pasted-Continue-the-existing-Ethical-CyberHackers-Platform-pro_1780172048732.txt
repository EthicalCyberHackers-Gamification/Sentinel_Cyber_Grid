Now implement:

Milestone 30A — Persistent Adversary Presence

Purpose:
Make Red Team feel like an active opposing force throughout the mission instead of occasional scripted events. The student should feel like an attacker is continuously attempting to adapt and progress against the organization.

Important:
Do NOT add real hacking.
Do NOT add backend.
Do NOT add AI.
Do NOT add multiplayer.
Do NOT create complicated strategy systems.
Do NOT overwhelm students.

Core Design Goal:
Students should feel:

“Blue Team is actively responding to a persistent attacker.”

NOT:
“I am completing isolated lesson steps.”

---

1. ADD PERSISTENT RED TEAM STATE

---

Create lightweight adversary state tracking.

Add reusable functions:

updateRedTeamActivity()
triggerRedTeamMovement()
renderAdversaryStatus()

Purpose:
The attacker should appear continuously active in the background of the mission.

---

2. ADD RED TEAM STATUS PANEL

---

Add compact section in Live Incident Intelligence region:

RED TEAM ACTIVITY

Possible states:

* Recon Activity Detected
* Credential Harvesting Attempt
* Suspicious Outbound Traffic
* Threat Expanding
* Containment Pressure Rising
* Activity Stabilized

Display:

* compact
* operational
* continuously alive

Do NOT create giant dashboards.

---

3. ADD ADVERSARY ADAPTATION EVENTS

---

If Blue Team contains one threat path:
Red Team should appear to adapt.

Examples:

After workstation isolation:
⚠ RED TEAM ADAPTATION
Suspicious activity shifting to alternate employee account.

After delayed escalation:
⚠ ATTACKER MOVEMENT
Credential harvesting attempts increasing.

After strong containment:
✔ RED TEAM ACTIVITY REDUCED
Threat activity partially stabilized.

Purpose:
Attacker should feel reactive and persistent.

---

4. ADD RED TEAM OBJECTIVE DISCOVERY

---

As investigation progresses, student gradually uncovers likely attacker intent.

Examples:
Possible Adversary Goal:

* Credential Theft
* Unauthorized Access
* Phishing Expansion
* Internal Reconnaissance

Reveal gradually through investigation progress.

Do NOT dump all information immediately.

---

5. ADD BACKGROUND OPERATIONAL ACTIVITY

---

Occasionally show subtle operational updates.

Examples:
“Email filtering rules updating...”
“Security monitoring elevated.”
“SOC analysts reviewing outbound traffic.”
“Finance department notified of phishing risk.”

These should:

* appear subtly
* feel environmental
* make the organization feel alive

Do NOT create giant feeds.

---

6. ADD THREAT PRESSURE RHYTHM

---

Red Team activity should fluctuate based on Blue Team decisions.

Correct containment:

* activity stabilizes
* threat pressure lowers

Delayed response:

* activity increases
* escalation events become more serious

Poor decision:

* attacker activity spreads slightly

Do NOT heavily punish students.

---

7. ADD RED TEAM VISUAL IDENTITY

---

Red Team events should have subtle visual identity:

* red accent glow
* threat pulse
* adversary iconography
* operational warning styling

Keep professional and lightweight.

Do NOT become cartoonish.
