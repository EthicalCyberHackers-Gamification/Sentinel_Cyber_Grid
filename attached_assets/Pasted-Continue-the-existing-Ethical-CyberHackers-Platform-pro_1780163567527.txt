Now implement:

Milestone 28B — Reactive Incident Evolution

Purpose:
Make the cyber incident evolve in response to student decisions. The student should feel like the investigation is unfolding dynamically rather than following a fixed script.

Important:
Do NOT redesign the app.
Do NOT add backend.
Do NOT add AI.
Do NOT add multiplayer.
Do NOT create giant branching systems.
Do NOT overwhelm students.

Core Design Goal:
The incident should react BELIEVABLY to Blue Team decisions.

The player should feel:
“My earlier actions changed what happened next.”

---

1. ADD INCIDENT EVOLUTION SYSTEM

---

Add a lightweight reactive incident system.

Create reusable functions:

triggerIncidentEvolution(eventType)
advanceIncidentState()
renderIncidentTimelineUpdate()

Purpose:
The incident changes slightly depending on:

* containment choices
* escalation timing
* evidence quality
* operational decisions

---

2. DECISION-BASED INCIDENT REACTIONS

---

If student chooses:
Continue gathering evidence silently

Then later:
Trigger:

[RED TEAM ACTIVITY]
Additional suspicious outbound activity detected.

Manager:
“The threat may be spreading beyond the original workstation.”

Effects:

* Incident Pressure rises slightly
* Threat Level pulses briefly

---

3. FAST ESCALATION RESPONSE

---

If student chooses:
Escalate immediately

Later:
Trigger:

[BLUE TEAM UPDATE]
Lead analyst reviewing containment request.

Then:
[INCIDENT UPDATE]
Potential credential spread interrupted early.

Manager:
“Strong escalation timing helped reduce operational risk.”

Effects:

* Manager Trust increases
* Containment Progress improves

---

4. WORKSTATION ISOLATION RESPONSE

---

If student chooses:
Isolate workstation

Later:
Trigger:

[BLUE TEAM ACTION]
Workstation isolation successful.

Then:
[INCIDENT UPDATE]
External communication attempts reduced.

Manager:
“Containment reduced threat spread, though some evidence collection was limited.”

Effects:

* Threat Level decreases
* Containment Progress increases strongly
* Analyst Confidence slightly reduced or stabilized

---

5. IGNORE OPTION RESPONSE

---

If student chooses:
Ignore for now

Later:
Trigger:

[RED TEAM MOVEMENT]
Additional employee targeted by suspicious email activity.

Manager:
“We may now be dealing with wider phishing exposure.”

Effects:

* Threat Level increases
* Incident Pressure rises
* Trust Score decreases slightly

Do NOT heavily punish the student.

---

6. ADD REACTIVE INCIDENT TIMELINE

---

Expand the compact timeline.

Examples:

09:12 Incident detected
09:14 Analyst assigned
09:16 Suspicious file identified
09:18 Workstation isolated
09:19 Additional outbound activity detected
09:21 Escalation initiated

Timeline entries should change depending on student decisions.

Keep timeline compact and readable.

---

7. ADD SMALL ENVIRONMENTAL REACTIONS

---

When reactive events occur:

* current mission node pulses briefly
* threat indicator animates slightly
* containment progress updates visibly
* event toast appears

Do NOT add giant popups.

Do NOT interrupt gameplay heavily.

---

8. ADD MANAGER EMOTIONAL REACTIONS

---

Manager messages should feel more human and reactive.

Examples:

Positive:
“Good judgment. That helped stabilize the situation.”

Concerned:
“We may be losing containment.”

Neutral:
“Continue carefully. We still need stronger evidence.”

Do NOT use AI.
Keep scripted reactions.

---

9. ADD REACTIVE MISSION OUTCOMES

---

Mission end summaries should vary based on student decisions.

Examples:

Excellent Containment
“Threat contained before additional spread.”

Delayed Containment
“Threat contained after limited phishing expansion.”

Reactive Recovery
“Threat stabilized after operational escalation.”

Weak Response
“Incident escalated after delayed Blue Team action.”

Do NOT fail the mission completely.

Mission 1 should now feel like a living cyber incident where:

* student decisions influence the environment
* Blue Team actions affect containment
