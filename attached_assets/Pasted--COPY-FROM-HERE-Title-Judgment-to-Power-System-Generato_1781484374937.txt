Title: Judgment-to-Power System Generator — CyberCorp

Task
Design systems that convert demonstrated analyst judgment into small, tactile, spendable powers without undermining professional identity. Outputs must be diegetic to CyberCorp and reinforce the Discover → Interpret → Justify → Learn loop.

Goals
• Reward correct or well‑reasoned judgments with precise, actionable affordances (not points).
• Keep powers lightweight, time‑bound, and situational; they should accelerate thinking, not replace it.
• Make tradeoffs visible: spending power should carry a small, teachable cost.
• Preserve identity: rewards feel like earned trust/tools, not game loot.

Constraints
• Tone: professional, grounded, in‑universe. Avoid overt gamification language.
• Powers must be triggered by recognizable judgment patterns (e.g., accurate risk calls, strong justification use), not grind.
• Each power must specify: trigger condition, effect, duration/limits, and a subtle cost or counterweight.
• No permanent stat increases; no leaderboards; no RNG loot.
• Integrate with existing systems when relevant: Investigation Confidence, Sarah Reyes, Notebook, Case Board.
• Design for clarity and teachability in 1–2 sentences per effect.

Inputs (provided to you)
• Mission context (1–3 sentences)
• Role: Intern | Junior SOC Analyst | SOC Analyst
• Judgment pattern(s) to reward (e.g., “accurate medium-risk calls”, “declaring and resolving unknowns”)
• Desired power category (choose one or more): Hinting | Acceleration | Context Reveal | Risk Calibration | Evidence Surfacing
• Strictness: Lenient | Standard | Exacting (affects trigger thresholds and costs)

Design rules
• Powers accelerate analyst behavior the player already demonstrated; they never reveal answers outright.
• Tie each power to a single, named judgment habit; state the teaching intent explicitly via its trigger and cost.
• Costs should teach craft: small Confidence spend, stricter next ambiguity, or minor Operational Friction—never punitive.
• Keep names credible (e.g., “Evidence Threader”, “Scope Snapshot”, “Risk Railguard”), not gamey.
• Ensure at least one power interfaces with Investigation Confidence as a limited, spendable resource.
