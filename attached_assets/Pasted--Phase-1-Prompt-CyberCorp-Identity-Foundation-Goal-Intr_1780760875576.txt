# Phase 1 Prompt — CyberCorp Identity Foundation

Goal:
Introduce persistent organizational identity without changing the current gameplay loop or mission systems.

This phase is NOT a redesign of gameplay mechanics.

This phase exists to solve immersion continuity and establish:

* who the player is
* where the player works
* why assignments matter

The player should begin feeling like:
“I work inside a real cybersecurity operations environment.”

Keep all current missions, terminal mechanics, and gameplay systems intact.

Do not introduce new gameplay complexity in this phase.

---

## WHAT STAYS THE SAME

Keep:

* current world map layout
* current mission progression
* current terminal system
* current mission logic
* current scoring systems
* current command systems
* current evidence systems
* current assignment launch flow

Do NOT redesign the missions yet.

Do NOT add new gameplay mechanics yet.

Do NOT clutter the interface with corporate information.

This phase is identity and immersion layering only.

---

## WHY THIS FEATURE IS BEING ADDED

Current issue:

The player performs cybersecurity tasks, but lacks persistent organizational identity.

Example:
In Assignment 001, the phishing email references CyberCorp, but the player may not understand:

* that CyberCorp is their employer
* that the email targets their own organization
* that they are defending a company they belong to

As a result:

* investigations feel isolated
* alerts feel abstract
* the company lacks emotional meaning
* missions feel disconnected from each other

This phase fixes that by introducing:
persistent organizational continuity.

The goal is NOT cinematic storytelling.

The goal is:
operational immersion.

---

## PHASE 1 IMPLEMENTATION

Add a lightweight persistent identity system.

The player should always know:

* employer
* division
* current role
* supervisor
* current clearance/access level

---

## OPERATIONS CENTER ADDITIONS

Add a subtle persistent identity panel somewhere appropriate in the Operations Center/world map interface.

Example:

CyberCorp
Security Operations Division

Role:
Cybersecurity Intern

Supervisor:
Analyst Reynolds

Clearance:
Tier 1 Operations Access

Do NOT make this large or distracting.

This should feel like:
an employee operations dashboard.

---

## MISSION LAUNCH ADDITIONS

Before a mission launches, add a short operational briefing.

Keep it concise.

Example:

CyberCorp Security Operations
Assignment 001 — Credential Phishing Campaign

Finance reported suspicious emails impersonating CyberCorp branding.

As a Cybersecurity Intern, your task is to inspect the email artifact, identify suspicious indicators, and escalate confirmed evidence.

The purpose is:

* establish context
* establish role
* establish operational responsibility

---

## MISSION HEADER ADDITIONS

Inside the mission:
add small contextual references only.

Examples:

* CyberCorp branding
* department names
* ticket IDs
* assigned analyst queue
* supervisor references

Do NOT overload the mission UI.

Mission screen must remain focused on:

* terminal
* evidence
* investigation

---

## SUPERVISOR CONTINUITY

Introduce recurring supervisors.

Example:
Analyst Reynolds appears repeatedly across missions.

This creates:

* continuity
* mentorship feeling
* operational realism

Keep this subtle.

Avoid heavy dialogue systems.

---

## EMPLOYEE CONTINUITY

Some employees should recur across missions.

Example:
A finance employee from Mission 001 later appears:

* in another phishing campaign
* in access logs
* in account alerts
* in incident reports

This creates:
persistent world continuity.

---

## DESIGN RULES

Do NOT:

* turn the game into story mode
* add long cutscenes
* add cinematic dialogue sequences
* interrupt gameplay flow
* clutter the UI

The immersion should come from:
working inside a believable organization.

NOT from scripted narrative sequences.

---

## SUCCESS CRITERIA

This phase is successful if:

* the player understands they work for CyberCorp
* missions feel connected
* assignments feel operationally meaningful
* the player understands their role inside the company
* immersion improves without increasing clutter
* gameplay speed remains fast
* the current mission systems remain intact
