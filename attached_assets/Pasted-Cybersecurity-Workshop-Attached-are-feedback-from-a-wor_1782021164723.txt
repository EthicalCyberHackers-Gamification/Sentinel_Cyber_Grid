Cybersecurity Workshop: Attached are feedback from a workshop conducted on June 20th 2026. Can you help generate ideas for game improvement for Replit


Yes - here are concrete game improvement ideas pulled from the whiteboard feedback, organized into themes and translated into product changes for a Replit-based cybersecurity workshop game.

What the feedback is saying
The strongest signals on the board appear to be:

Workshop goals were not clear at the beginning
Instructions / mission goals were unclear
Visual clarity was weak: grey font hard to read, too much happening on screen
Opening files created confusion
The right-side column felt low-value
Players wanted clearer feedback after each mission
Engagement / entertainment / educational value were mixed, with room to improve
Voice-over and video may have helped clarity
Difficulty and pacing need tuning
That points to one main diagnosis:

The game likely has a good concept, but users are losing confidence because the experience is not clear enough early, and the interface is making learning harder instead of easier.

Top improvement priorities
If Replit wants the fastest gains, I would prioritize these in order:

Priority	Improvement area	Why it matters
1	Clear onboarding and workshop framing	Confusion at the start poisons the whole session
2	Mission clarity and step-by-step guidance	Users need to know what to do next at all times
3	UI simplification and readability	Cognitive overload is hurting learning
4	Stronger mission-end feedback	Users want to know how they performed
5	Better pacing and engagement	Makes the workshop feel more rewarding and less fatiguing

5. Add a dedicated file-inspection view
The feedback suggests that players are struggling to identify which information in the terminal is the actual evidence they need to review. While the file content is technically visible, it blends into the surrounding terminal output, making it hard for new players to distinguish critical clues from general interface noise.

Improvements
When a player opens a mission-related file, display it in a visually distinct inspection layer rather than inline with the rest of the terminal content. This could take the form of a:

modal pop-up
side panel
full-screen evidence viewer
The goal is to present the exact file content in a cleaner, more readable surface that makes its importance immediately obvious.

Product idea
Create an Evidence Viewer that appears whenever a player opens a file tied to a mission. The viewer should:

clearly label the file as mission evidence
show the exact file contents without blending into terminal chatter
use stronger contrast, spacing, and framing for readability
include a short prompt explaining that this is the information the player needs to inspect
allow the player to close the view and return to the terminal easily
Why this matters
Right now, players may be spending effort figuring out what they are supposed to look at instead of analyzing the cybersecurity content itself. A dedicated file-inspection view reduces that friction and helps players focus on the actual task: reviewing evidence, spotting anomalies, and making decisions.

Example copy
Evidence File: Suspicious Login Report
Review this file carefully. It contains information needed to complete the mission.

This is especially valuable in a Replit-based terminal experience, where preserving realism is useful, but not at the expense of clarity. The file should remain authentic, but its presentation should make it unmistakably clear that it is the key artifact for investigation.
