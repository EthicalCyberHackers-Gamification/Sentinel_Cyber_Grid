One-screen acceptance checklist — #116 Analyst Notebook readability redesign

Objective
Ensure the right-column investigation pane is clear, scannable, and consistent post-redesign (Live Comms + Mark‑up + Findings).

Acceptance criteria (all must pass)
1) Visual hierarchy
• Each finding/thread has a clear title style distinct from meta text.
• Consistent spacing between findings/threads (at least 12–16px vertical rhythm).
• Section dividers or subtle rules visually group related content without clutter.

2) Typography and affordances
• Type scale: titles > body > meta/chips; no collisions or cramped lines.
• Chips (actor/action/asset/risk) are visually distinct, tappable/clickable, and have visible focus states.
• Link-like elements (e.g., “Derived from Decision …”) are styled as actions and discoverable.

3) Empty and edge states
• Empty notebook/first finding: displays a short, in-world prompt (no quiz/test language) that points to the next action (e.g., “Highlight what stands out in the artifact to start your case thread.”).
• No-data states avoid red error chrome; use calm explanatory copy.
• Overflow handling: long artifact titles or chip values truncate gracefully with tooltips.

4) Feedback and guidance (readability, not grading)
• Confidence nudge and Evidence Breadth dots are visible but unobtrusive; no numeric grades.
• Sarah Comms is visually anchored and does not scroll out of context during short interactions.

5) Accessibility
• Keyboard: tab order reaches findings, chips, and “Commit” in a logical sequence.
• Visible focus ring on interactive elements (chips, links, highlights).
• Screen-reader labels exist for chips and “Derived from Decision …” links (role + name).

6) Responsiveness
• At 900–1024px widths, layout maintains hierarchy without overlap.
• At 1280px+, line lengths remain readable; no ultra-wide paragraphs.

Verification steps (10 minutes)

• Trigger an empty state (new mission) and confirm copy and CTA.
• Navigate purely via keyboard to commit a finding; observe focus order and ring.
