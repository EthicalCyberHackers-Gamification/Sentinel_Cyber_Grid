Title: Replace Graded Notebook with Live Comms + Inline Evidence Mark‑up (Keep Scoring Intact)

Task
Redesign the right‑column “Analyst Notebook” so it feels like live investigation rather than a graded exam. Keep the existing judgment engine, scoring, confidence, and consequence logic untouched. Remove answer‑key UI (✓/✗, “correct answer”, “Partly right”). Route judgments through an in‑character Sarah Comms panel and inline evidence highlighting. Auto‑compose findings; avoid free‑form essay boxes.

Goals
• Preserve current correctness, confidence deltas, and persistence.
• Eliminate test‑like visuals and flows.
• Let players act on artifacts directly (highlight/mark), then state implications via live comms with Sarah.
• Generate concise, editable findings automatically from player actions.

Constraints
• No changes to backend judgment APIs (e.g., setDiscoveryJudgment), scoring rules, or data schema.
• No correctness reveal in UI (no ✓/✗, no “correct answer” text).
• Tone: professional, in‑universe; mentorship over grading.

Inputs (use existing sources)
• Current judgment prompts and options.
• Outcome enums mapping to confidence/consequence updates.
• Artifact viewers (terminal/log/email/filemeta) content.

Output (deliverables)
1) Comms renderer
• Present judgment prompts as Sarah messages.
• Render options as first‑person response buttons the player “says”.
• On select: call existing grading function; do not reveal correctness.
• Map outcome to one short Sarah line (Coaching | Calibration | Context).

2) Artifact inline mark‑up
• Enable text selection in artifact viewers.
• On selection: show a tag popover with Fact | Anomaly | Unknown.
• Tagging Anomaly triggers the “What stands out?” judgment (context = selected snippet).
• Immediately open the follow‑up “Why does it matter?” in Comms.
• Persist a subtle, labeled highlight; clicking it reopens the related decision.

3) Auto‑compose Finding blocks
• After each interpret/justify step, generate a 1–2 sentence finding with editable chips:
  - Template examples:
    - “Anomaly: [actor] [action] [asset] without [control] during [timewindow] — risk [level].”
    - “Interpretation: Evidence suggests [cause] impacting [businessarea].”
• Commit writes to the existing notebook/record schema. No blank textarea.

4) Diegetic feedback signals (replace grades)
• Confidence micro‑nudge (+/‑) animation only.
• Evidence Diversity dots (0–3) fill as distinct sources support a finding.
• Optional Operational Friction micro‑bar rises on over‑escalation choices.
• No “correct/incorrect/partly right” labels anywhere.

5) Optional “Analyst’s Bet” (once per mission)
• Sarah offers an optional hypothesis check: “If your read is X, which disconfirming evidence would you expect?”
• Strong pick: reveal a scoped “Scope Snapshot” artifact at a small Confidence spend.
• Weak pick: brief coaching line. Entirely optional, no gating.

Acceptance criteria
• Players can complete a finding with ≤15 typed words and never see an answer key.
• Time from first highlight to committed finding ≤90 seconds once artifacts are present.
• Confidence and consequence systems update exactly as before.
• Qual feedback: players describe it as “working a case,” not “taking a test.”

Implementation notes
• Do not alter correctness logic or persistence; only change renderers and interactions.
• Comms outcome mapping: provide 12–18 Sarah one‑liners keyed to existing outcome enums (strong/near‑miss/miss).
• Chip vocab: supply mission‑specific values for [actor], [action], [asset], [control], [timewindow], [level], [cause], [businessarea].
• Accessibility: keyboard navigation for highlight→tag→commit flows.

Telemetry to log
• Time to first committed finding after artifact availability.
• Ratio of highlights leading to decisions vs. abandoned highlights.
• Evidence source count per committed finding.
• Confidence spends (if using Analyst’s Bet), and post‑spend accuracy.

Now build.
