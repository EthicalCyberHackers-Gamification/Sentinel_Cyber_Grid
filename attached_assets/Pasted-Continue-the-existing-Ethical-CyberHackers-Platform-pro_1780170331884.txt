Now implement:

Milestone 29A — Cyber Operations Center Spatial Layout

Purpose:
Gradually transform the interface from a “web dashboard” feeling into a believable cybersecurity operations center environment WITHOUT disrupting the existing architecture or overwhelming students.

Important:
Do NOT redesign the entire application.
Do NOT break existing systems.
Do NOT add giant new panels.
Do NOT create clutter.
Do NOT move core gameplay away from the terminal investigation flow.

Core Design Goal:
The interface should psychologically feel like:
a live cybersecurity operations center.

NOT:
a learning website with stacked boxes.

---

1. ESTABLISH SPATIAL HIERARCHY

---

Restructure the layout into 3 psychological regions:

LEFT SIDE
Mission Operations

CENTER
Active Investigation Space

RIGHT SIDE
Live Incident Intelligence

Do NOT fully rebuild architecture.
Repurpose and reorganize existing sections gradually.

---

2. CENTER = ACTIVE INVESTIGATION SPACE

---

The CENTER area must become the visual focus of the entire interface.

This region should contain:

* terminal
* active file investigation
* operational decisions
* reasoning prompts
* cinematic interruptions

Visual behavior:

* slightly brighter contrast
* subtle operational glow
* strongest visual focus

Purpose:
This becomes the “live incident battlefield.”

---

3. LEFT SIDE = MISSION OPERATIONS

---

Move or organize these systems into the left operational region:

* Mission Route / Mission Nodes
* Blue Team Status
* Manager Transmission
* Containment Progress
* Current Assignment

This side should feel like:
operations coordination.

Reduce excessive spacing and stacked-card appearance.

Make sections feel more integrated.

---

4. RIGHT SIDE = LIVE INCIDENT INTELLIGENCE

---

Move or organize these systems into the right intelligence region:

* Threat Level
* Analyst Confidence
* Investigation Timeline
* Evidence Board
* Red Team Activity
* Trust Score

This side should feel like:
live cyber incident monitoring.

Use compact information density.

Avoid giant card stacks.

---

5. REDUCE “WEBSITE PANEL” FEEL

---

Current issue:
Too many boxed panels create a traditional dashboard appearance.

Reduce:

* heavy borders
* excessive outlines
* thick card separation
* isolated rectangles

Increase:

* layered transparency
* subtle connected backgrounds
* integrated operational surfaces
* shared spatial grouping

Goal:
The interface should feel continuous and operational.

---

6. ADD PERSISTENT OPERATIONS STRIP

---

Add a compact persistent top operations strip.

Example content:
BLUE TEAM ACTIVE
INCIDENT STATUS: INVESTIGATING
THREAT LEVEL: MEDIUM
CONTAINMENT: PARTIAL

Behavior:

* subtle
* always visible
* compact
* operational tone

Do NOT make it flashy.

---

7. ENVIRONMENTAL DENSITY REACTIONS

---

The environment should subtly react depending on context.

Examples:

Investigation active:
Center region brightens slightly.

Threat escalation:
Right intelligence region pulses subtly.

Mission complete:
Mission Operations region glows softly.

Containment success:
Containment indicator stabilizes visually.

Keep all effects:
lightweight,
professional,
and cinematic.

---

8. MAKE THE WORLD FEEL ACTIVE

---

Add small environmental operational updates.

Examples:
“Security monitoring elevated.”
“Lead analyst reviewing escalation.”
“Finance department notified.”
“Email gateway filtering suspicious traffic.”

These should appear:
occasionally,
subtly,
and compactly.

Do NOT create giant feeds.

Purpose:
The organization should feel alive.

---

9. IMPROVE MISSION NODE IMMERSION

---

Mission nodes should feel like active operational sectors.

Locked missions should still appear alive.

Example:
Mission 3:
Reconnaissance Activity Detected
Status: Monitoring

Do NOT unlock Mission 3 yet.

Purpose:
Create anticipation and operational continuity.

---

10. MAINTAIN LOW COGNITIVE LOAD

---

Do NOT:

* increase visible complexity
* add more commands
* add giant dashboards
* show many simultaneous alerts
* overload students with data

The interface should feel:
clean,
focused,
and immersive.

---

11. PRESERVE EXISTING SYSTEMS

---

Do not break:

* terminal commands
* guided file reveal
* reasoning prompts
* evidence classification
* decision system
* reactive incident evolution
* cinematic interruptions
* manager reactions
* hints
* XP
* trust score
* threat level
* containment progress
* mission completion
* scorecards
* mission map
* local progress save
* Mission 2

---

## GOAL

The interface should now feel like:

a lightweight cinematic cybersecurity operations center

WITHOUT:
