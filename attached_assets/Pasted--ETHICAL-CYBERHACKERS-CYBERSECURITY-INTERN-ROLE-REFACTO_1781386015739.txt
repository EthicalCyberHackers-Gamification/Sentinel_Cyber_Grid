IMPORTANT

Do NOT redesign the UI.

Do NOT replace the Operating Center.

Do NOT remove the Terminal.

Do NOT remove the Analyst Notebook.

Do NOT remove Investigation Confidence.

Do NOT remove Resource Tracking.

Do NOT remove Consequence Tracking.

Do NOT add additional Intern missions.

The current UI foundation is strong.

The curriculum philosophy must change.

The Intern role is no longer a collection of cybersecurity lessons.

The Intern role becomes:

"A student's first month working inside a Security Operations Center."

The curriculum should teach students how cybersecurity professionals think, investigate, document, escalate, and assess risk.

Students should not feel like they are solving puzzles.

Students should feel like they are performing real cybersecurity work.

--------------------------------------------------
ROLE DESIGN PHILOSOPHY
--------------------------------------------------

The Cybersecurity Intern is NOT learning:

- Tools
- Commands
- Memorization
- Attack terminology

The Cybersecurity Intern IS learning:

- Observation
- Investigation
- Evidence Collection
- Documentation
- Escalation
- Risk Recognition

Students should continuously ask:

What happened?

What evidence supports it?

How serious is it?

Who should know?

What should happen next?

--------------------------------------------------
AUTHORITY MODEL
--------------------------------------------------

Cybersecurity Intern CAN:

- Investigate
- Classify
- Recommend
- Escalate
- Document
- Submit Findings

Cybersecurity Intern CANNOT:

- Approve Budgets
- Purchase Tools
- Change Policy
- Approve Containment
- Hire Vendors
- Engage Executives
- Engage Legal Directly
- Notify Customers

Students should see these activities happen.

Students should not control them.

--------------------------------------------------
ANALYST NOTEBOOK REFACTOR
--------------------------------------------------

Replace checklist mentality.

Notebook Sections:

FACTS

HYPOTHESES

UNKNOWNS

RECOMMENDATIONS

--------------------------------------------------
FACTS
--------------------------------------------------

Only verified information.

Example:

✓ Contractor account accessed restricted files

✓ Unknown device exists

✓ Login succeeded after repeated failures

--------------------------------------------------
HYPOTHESES
--------------------------------------------------

Possible explanations.

Example:

□ Contractor account may be compromised

□ Device may be unauthorized

□ Login pattern may indicate account takeover

--------------------------------------------------
UNKNOWNS
--------------------------------------------------

Open questions.

Example:

□ Who owns the device?

□ Was access legitimate?

□ Was data transferred externally?

--------------------------------------------------
RECOMMENDATIONS
--------------------------------------------------

Student-generated actions.

Example:

□ Escalate

□ Recommend MFA

□ Recommend legal review

□ Restrict access
