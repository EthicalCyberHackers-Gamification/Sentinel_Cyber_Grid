Build a cybersecurity simulation game mission framework focused on a stronger emotional and cognitive loop: curiosity, uncertainty, judgment, consequence, and payoff.

## Goal
Increase player engagement by making each mission feel like a living investigation where the player is rewarded for reasoning, ambiguity handling, and professional judgment rather than just finding the right answer.

## Core design requirements
- The game must center on a cybersecurity analyst identity.
- Each mission must follow a clear arc: setup, anomaly, tension, decision, consequence, resolution.
- The player should regularly face multiple plausible interpretations of the same evidence.
- The game should reward reasoning quality, not just final correctness.
- Sarah Reyes should function as an adaptive mentor who remembers the player’s reasoning style.
- Mission outcomes should create visible and human-feeling organizational consequences.

## Strategy to implement
1. Design every mission around a sharp emotional arc.
2. Make uncertainty the main source of fun.
3. Reward reasoning quality more than correctness.
4. Turn Sarah into a real mentoring relationship with memory.
5. Add micro-payoffs every few minutes to keep momentum high.
6. Make consequences visible through company reactions and after-action updates.
7. Support player identity through analyst styles, heuristics, or role variants.
8. Use bounded tension: pressure should matter without feeling punishing.

## Product requirements
Create a mission template that supports:
- A clean starting context
- One obvious clue
- Two or three optional anomalies
- At least one judgment under incomplete information
- A counter-argument or “what else could this be?” prompt
- A mission-end consequence update
- A short Sarah recap that references the player’s reasoning

## UX and feedback requirements
- Show ambiguity clearly when it exists.
- Prompt the player to think before answering.
- Capture the player’s confidence and rationale.
- Track the quality of evidence used.
- Give Sarah memory-based feedback later in the mission or in future missions.
- Deliver small rewards frequently so the player feels steady progress.

## Success criteria
The experience should make the player feel:
- curious about what the clue means
- confident that their reasoning matters
- recognized by Sarah and the organization
- motivated to continue the next mission
- able to learn from uncertainty rather than avoid it

## Output expected
Generate:
- a mission design framework
- a list of the core mechanics needed
- the flow of a single mission using this strategy
- UI prompts that support curiosity, uncertainty, and judgment
- example Sarah feedback lines
- example mission consequence updates

## Constraints
- Keep the design professional and cybersecurity-themed.
- Avoid arcade-style scoring.
- Avoid overloading the player with too many systems at once.
- Prioritize engagement through pacing, recognition, and meaningful judgment.
