# Player Cognition Layer — Phased Implementation Plan

## Psychological Investigation Architecture for Ethical CyberHackers

Goal:
Transform the simulator from:
“command progression gameplay”

into:

“investigative analyst cognition training.”

The player should feel:

* suspicion
* curiosity
* uncertainty
* investigative momentum
* operational responsibility
* discovery satisfaction

The simulator should teach:
how analysts think.

NOT just:
what commands analysts type.

This system must preserve:

* immersion
* realism
* operational atmosphere
* terminal-first gameplay
* reactive maps
* evidence pinning
* CyberCorp identity

Do NOT redesign the core simulator.

This is an additive cognitive framework layered onto the existing architecture.

---

## CORE EDUCATIONAL PHILOSOPHY

Current risk:
Players can complete missions procedurally without fully understanding:

* what question they are trying to answer
* why evidence matters
* why commands exist
* what conclusions analysts are forming
* why containment actions are logical

The simulator must shift from:

command
→ output
→ next command

to:

suspicion
→ investigative question
→ tool selection
→ evidence
→ interpretation
→ operational conclusion
→ response action

The player should feel:
“I am uncovering a living cyber incident.”

NOT:
“I am executing hidden command triggers.”

---

## OVERALL IMPLEMENTATION STRATEGY

Implement this system in phases.

Do NOT attempt a full redesign at once.

Prioritize:

* educational clarity
* operational immersion
* psychological engagement
* minimal disruption to current flow

---

## PHASE 1 — INVESTIGATIVE FRAMING LAYER

Priority:
CRITICAL

Goal:
Ensure the player always understands:

* what suspicious condition exists
* what investigative question they are trying to answer
* why the next action matters

---

## CURRENT PROBLEM

Players often receive:
commands or hints

without enough:
investigative framing.

This creates:
mechanical progression.

---

## IMPLEMENTATION

Before each meaningful stage:
show a short operational framing block.

Structure:

CURRENT SUSPICION
INVESTIGATIVE QUESTION
WHY THIS MATTERS

---

## EXAMPLE

CURRENT SUSPICION
An external source is touching services this host does not normally expose.

INVESTIGATIVE QUESTION
Determine whether the source belongs to legitimate infrastructure or unauthorized reconnaissance activity.

WHY THIS MATTERS
Reconnaissance often appears before intrusion attempts. Analysts validate ownership and traffic behavior before escalating.

---

## IMPORTANT RULES

Keep:

* concise
* operational
* immersive
* realistic

Avoid:

* tutorial tone
* classroom language
* long paragraphs
* excessive hand-holding

---

## SUCCESS CRITERIA

Players should understand:

* why they are investigating
* what they are trying to prove
* why the command matters

before using the tool.

---

## PHASE 2 — COMMAND PURPOSE & ANALYST THINKING

Priority:
CRITICAL

Goal:
Commands should feel like:
tools for answering investigative questions.

NOT:
required puzzle inputs.

---

## IMPLEMENTATION

When commands/tools are introduced:
briefly explain their investigative purpose.

---

## EXAMPLES

WHOIS
Used to determine who owns infrastructure and whether it belongs to known legitimate organizations.

PORT ANALYSIS
Used to identify whether communication matches expected services or broad reconnaissance behavior.

BASELINE COMPARE
Used to determine whether activity matches known-good operational behavior.

HEADERS
Used to validate whether email infrastructure is authorized by the claimed sender.

---

## IMPORTANT DESIGN PRINCIPLE

Do NOT over-explain syntax.

Focus on:
investigative reasoning.

The player should learn:
why analysts use tools.

---

## PHASE 3 — EVIDENCE INTERPRETATION LAYER

Priority:
HIGHEST

Goal:
Ensure players understand:
what evidence means operationally.

---

## CURRENT PROBLEM

Players can pin evidence without understanding:

* why it matters
* what conclusion it supports
* how it affects confidence

---

## IMPLEMENTATION

Every major evidence item should contain:

WHAT WAS FOUND
WHY IT MATTERS
WHAT CONCLUSION IT SUPPORTS

---

## EXAMPLE

EVIDENCE
Repeated probing of SSH, HTTP, HTTPS, and database services.

WHY IT MATTERS
Legitimate systems usually communicate with expected services only. Broad probing across unrelated ports often indicates automated discovery activity.

SUPPORTS CONCLUSION
External reconnaissance behavior.

---

## IMPORTANT RULE

Evidence should:
increase or decrease investigative confidence.

The player should feel:
the investigation is evolving.

---

## PHASE 4 — COGNITIVE TENSION SYSTEM

Priority:
HIGH

Goal:
Create psychological investigative engagement.

The player should feel:
“Something is wrong, but I need proof.”

---

## IMPLEMENTATION PRINCIPLES

Do NOT:

* add artificial timers
* add arcade panic systems
* overload the screen
* constantly scream danger

Instead:
use uncertainty and progressive revelation.

---

## KEY MECHANICS

1. PARTIAL VISIBILITY

Do not reveal attacks immediately.

Reveal:

* anomalies
* suspicious indicators
* fragments
* inconsistencies

Allow the player to connect them.

---

2. HYPOTHESIS TESTING

Encourage:
“Is this benign or malicious?”

NOT:
“What command unlocks the next stage?”

---

3. ESCALATION

Discoveries should expand:

* maps
* attack scope
* compromised accounts
* infrastructure relationships
* incident understanding

---

4. INFORMATION GAPS

Always leave:
one unresolved question.

Example:
“The source is suspicious, but is this isolated or part of a broader campaign?”

---

5. CONSEQUENCE

Containment should visibly change the environment.

Examples:

* connections sever
* beacon stops
* compromised account stabilizes
* campaign spread halts

---

## SUCCESS CRITERIA

Players should feel:

* curiosity
* uncertainty
* urgency
* momentum
* discovery satisfaction

without losing operational realism.

---

## PHASE 5 — ANALYST VERIFICATION MOMENTS

Priority:
HIGH

Goal:
Prevent blind progression without breaking immersion.

---

## IMPORTANT RULE

Do NOT implement:
school quizzes.

Use:
analyst verification prompts.

---

## EXAMPLE

ANALYST VERIFICATION

Why is this suspicious?

A. The email arrived during business hours
B. SPF failed and the sender infrastructure is unauthorized
C. The subject line is short
D. The message used formal language

---

## AFTER RESPONSE

Correct.

SPF verifies whether infrastructure is authorized to send for a domain. Failed SPF strongly suggests spoofing or unauthorized mail infrastructure.

---

## RULES

* maximum 1–3 per mission
* major concept moments only
* operational tone only
* short explanations only
* no punishment loops
* no arcade scoring

---

## PHASE 6 — ACRONYM & VOCABULARY SUPPORT

Priority:
HIGH

Goal:
Reduce terminology overload without reducing realism.

---

## IMPLEMENTATION

Add:

* explain <term> command
* hover explanations
* contextual analyst notes
* CyberCorp knowledge base

---

## EXAMPLES

$ explain spf

Sender Policy Framework (SPF)

A DNS-based email validation mechanism used to determine whether a server is authorized to send email for a domain.

Failed SPF may indicate spoofing.

---

## IMPORTANT RULE

Keep:
real SOC terminology.

Support it contextually.

Do NOT replace it with simplified fake terminology.

---

## PHASE 7 — SOC DEBRIEF SYSTEM

Priority:
MEDIUM

Goal:
Convert mission completion into retained understanding.

---

## IMPLEMENTATION

At mission completion:
generate a short operational debrief.

Structure:

WHAT HAPPENED
HOW THE ATTACK WORKED
KEY EVIDENCE
WHY THE EVIDENCE MATTERED
CONTAINMENT IMPACT
REAL ANALYST TAKEAWAY

---

## EXAMPLE

This phishing campaign impersonated CyberCorp infrastructure using unauthorized mail servers and a lookalike domain. Analysts identified the campaign through SPF failure, suspicious links, and external infrastructure analysis.

Containment blocked communication with the phishing infrastructure and reduced further credential exposure.

---

## PHASE 8 — PLAYER PSYCHOLOGY OPTIMIZATION

Priority:
LATER

Goal:
Design the simulator around:
analyst cognitive development.

---

## TARGET PSYCHOLOGICAL PROGRESSION

Beginner:
“What am I supposed to do?”

↓

Curious Investigator:
“Something feels suspicious.”

↓

Evidence-Oriented Thinker:
“This evidence supports reconnaissance.”

↓

Operational Analyst:
“I need more proof before escalating.”

↓

Incident Responder:
“If I contain this infrastructure, the attack chain breaks.”

---

## IMPORTANT PRINCIPLE

The simulator should continuously build:

* confidence
* investigative intuition
* operational vocabulary
* analytical reasoning
* evidence-based thinking

The player should gradually internalize:
how cybersecurity analysts think.

---

## MOST IMPORTANT IMPLEMENTATION RULE

Do NOT sacrifice:

* immersion
* tension
* realism
* atmosphere
* operational pacing

The learning must stay embedded inside:
the investigation itself.

The strongest educational model is:

discover
→ interpret
→ understand
→ act
→ reflect

NOT:
read lesson
→ take quiz
→ continue.

---

## FINAL SUCCESS CRITERIA

This system succeeds if:

* players stop blindly following commands
* players understand investigative purpose
* players form hypotheses naturally
* players understand why evidence matters
* players gain operational confidence
* realism remains intact
* missions feel psychologically engaging
* the simulator teaches analyst thinking, not just task completion
