# ETHICAL CYBERHACKERS

# CAREER SIMULATION REBUILD

# MASTER REPLIT IMPLEMENTATION PROMPT

## PROJECT DIRECTION

We are refactoring the cybersecurity simulation into a role-based cybersecurity career simulator.

The objective is no longer to teach isolated cybersecurity concepts.

The objective is to simulate the responsibilities, decisions, tradeoffs, pressures, and consequences experienced by cybersecurity professionals throughout their careers.

The platform must start with the Cybersecurity Intern role but be architected from the beginning to support future career progression without requiring major rewrites.

Future roles will introduce increasing authority, budget responsibility, policy influence, executive communication, and organizational risk management.

Do not build future roles yet.

Build only the Cybersecurity Intern experience.

However, all architecture must be role-aware and future-ready.

# RESOURCE DEFINITIONS

## Security Posture

Represents organizational security health.

Range:

0-100

Higher is better.

---

## Organization Budget

Represents organizational spending capacity.

Used to demonstrate:

* legal costs
* incident response costs
* recovery costs
* consultant costs
* security investments

Interns cannot spend budget.

Interns can only affect budget through decisions.

---

## Executive Trust

Represents leadership confidence.

High trust improves future approvals.

Low trust restricts opportunities.

Range:

0-100

---

## Compliance Exposure

Represents legal and regulatory risk.

Range:

0-100

Lower is better.

---

## Career Reputation

Represents student progression.

Used for:

* promotions
* unlocks
* future role advancement

Range:

0-100

---

## Business Continuity

Represents operational stability.

Range:

0-100

Higher is better.

Security actions can improve security while reducing continuity.

Students must learn tradeoffs.
