PHASE 2 — Assignment 3: Reconnaissance Detection

Purpose:
Create the third Cybersecurity Intern assignment as a thrilling beginner-friendly threat-hunting experience.

This assignment should feel different from Assignment 1 and Assignment 2.

Assignment 1:
The player investigated a suspicious phishing file.

Assignment 2:
The player reviewed exposed network services.

Assignment 3:
The player detects signs that Red Team may be actively probing the organization before a larger attack.

Core Design Goal:
The student should feel:

“I am threat hunting. I am connecting weak signals before the attacker moves further.”

NOT:

“I am clicking another sequence of commands.”

---

1. ASSIGNMENT IDENTITY

---

Assignment title:
Reconnaissance Detection

Career role:
Cybersecurity Intern

Operational type:
Threat Hunting / Reconnaissance Monitoring

Threat theme:
External probing and early attacker discovery

Assignment story:
“Blue Team monitoring has detected unusual external activity around CyberCorp systems. No confirmed breach has occurred yet, but repeated connection attempts suggest Red Team may be mapping the environment. Your job is to investigate weak signals, identify whether reconnaissance is occurring, and recommend the correct Blue Team response.”

4. ASSIGNMENT 3 BRIEFING ROOM

---

Before investigation begins, show a briefing room with 3 short cards.

Card 1:
Reconnaissance

Content:

* Attackers often gather information before attacking.
* Recon activity may look like scans, probes, or repeated connection attempts.
* Early detection can prevent later compromise.

Card 2:
Weak Signals

Content:

* One event may not prove an attack.
* Several related events can reveal a pattern.
* Analysts look for repeated or unusual behavior.

Card 3:
Blue Team Response

Content:

* Gather evidence before escalating.
* Do not overreact to one weak signal.
* Recommend monitoring or hardening when patterns appear.

Student must review all briefing cards before launching the investigation.

Keep it brief and cinematic.

8. REASONING PROMPTS

---

After each major step, ask a short reasoning question.

After review alert:
Question:
“What does this alert suggest?”

Correct:
“Something unusual may be happening, but more evidence is needed.”

After check connections:
Question:
“What pattern do you notice?”

Correct:
“The same external source is contacting multiple services.”

After inspect source:
Question:
“Does this prove a breach?”

Correct:
“No. It suggests suspicious activity, but not confirmed compromise.”

After review service probes:
Question:
“Why are multiple service checks important?”

Correct:
“They may indicate an attacker mapping available services.”

After correlate findings:
Question:
“What is the strongest conclusion?”

Correct:
“This looks like possible reconnaissance activity.”

13. BLUE TEAM DECISION MOMENT

---

After correlate findings, show:

BLUE TEAM RESPONSE REQUIRED

Text:
“The same external source contacted multiple CyberCorp services. No breach is confirmed, but reconnaissance is likely. What should Blue Team do next?”

Options:
A. Escalate as possible reconnaissance and recommend monitoring/hardening
B. Ignore the activity because no breach is confirmed
C. Shut down all external services immediately
D. Delete the logs to reduce alert noise

Best answer:
A

Consequences:

A:

* Trust increases
* Analyst Confidence reaches Ready
* Containment/Readiness Progress increases
* Manager:
  “Good judgment. Reconnaissance should be escalated without overreacting.”

B:

* Threat pressure increases
* Manager:
  “Lack of confirmed breach does not mean activity should be ignored.”

C:

* Manager warns:
  “That response may be excessive without confirmed compromise.”

D:

* Strong warning:
  “Logs must be preserved. They are evidence.”

Do not fail the student completely.
Use feedback as teaching.
