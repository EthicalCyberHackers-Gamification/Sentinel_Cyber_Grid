# Phase 3 Prompt — Persistent Operational World & Reactive Organizational Environment

Goal:
Make CyberCorp feel like:
a living operational environment.

This phase creates:
persistent organizational memory across missions.

The world should feel:
connected, operational, and evolving.

NOT like isolated mission scenarios.

---

## WHAT STAYS THE SAME

Keep:

* current terminal architecture
* mission structure
* mission flow
* assignment logic
* operations center structure
* evidence systems
* investigation systems

Do NOT:

* create open-world gameplay
* create RPG dialogue systems
* add cinematic story mode
* interrupt investigation pacing

This phase enhances:
world continuity.

---

## WHY THIS FEATURE IS BEING ADDED

Current issue:

Missions feel self-contained.

After completion:
the world forgets what happened.

This weakens:

* realism
* continuity
* emotional investment
* organizational immersion

Real cybersecurity operations environments are persistent.

Events leave traces.

Employees recur.

Threats evolve.

Departments remember incidents.

This phase introduces:
persistent operational continuity.

---

## PERSISTENT EMPLOYEE SYSTEM

Employees should recur naturally.

Example:
An employee from Mission 001 later:

* reports another issue
* appears in access logs
* becomes phishing victim
* escalates suspicious activity

This creates:
organizational memory.

---

## PERSISTENT DEPARTMENTS

Create recurring departments:

* Finance
* HR
* Engineering
* Executive Operations
* IT Infrastructure
* Legal
* Security Operations

Missions should reference:
real departments consistently.

---

## SECURITY BULLETINS

Operations Center may occasionally show:
short security bulletins.

Examples:

* phishing warning
* suspicious activity notice
* infrastructure alert
* patch advisory

These should be lightweight and atmospheric.

Do NOT turn them into gameplay interruptions.

---

## REACTIVE WORLD STATE

Mission outcomes should occasionally affect future assignments.

Examples:

* blocked domain referenced later
* recurring attacker infrastructure
* repeat phishing campaigns
* compromised employee referenced again

This creates:
cause-and-effect continuity.

---

## RECURRING THREAT ACTORS

Introduce recurring adversary patterns.

Not named supervillains.

Instead:

* infrastructure reuse
* repeated domains
* repeated TTPs
* repeated malware family indicators

This creates:
realistic threat evolution.

---

## MISSION CONNECTIONS

Assignments should occasionally reference:
previous incidents.

Example:

“This domain resembles infrastructure seen in Assignment 003.”

This rewards:
player memory and operational awareness.

---

## DESIGN RULES

Keep:

* operational tone
* realism
* subtlety
* mission focus

Avoid:

* excessive exposition
* RPG systems
* branching dialogue trees
* cinematic storytelling
* clutter

The player should feel:
they are operating inside a persistent cybersecurity environment.

NOT:
playing through scripted story chapters.

---

## SUCCESS CRITERIA

This phase succeeds if:

* CyberCorp feels alive
* missions feel connected
* players remember employees and departments
* prior investigations feel meaningful
* operational realism increases
* immersion increases without slowing gameplay
* the game still prioritizes investigation and analysis above narrative
